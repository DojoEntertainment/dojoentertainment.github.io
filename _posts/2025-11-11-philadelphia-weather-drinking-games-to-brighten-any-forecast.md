Let’s be real: Philly weather is unpredictable, dramatic, and sometimes just plain weird. Sound familiar? It’s exactly the kind of energy you want for your next round of viral drinking games. From sudden storms to sunbursts, every weather shift is a chance for a new dare challenge—and maybe a few NSFW dares if you’re feeling brave.

## How To Play: Philadelphia Weather Drinking Game

**What you need:**
- Your favorite drinks (beer, seltzers, cocktails—whatever fits your vibe)
- A deck of Drinking Dojo weather cards (or use our [custom drinking game builder](https://drinkingdojo.com) to make your own)
- Optional: Weather app or live Philly weather report for real-time action

### Game Setup

1. **Deal the Weather Cards**  
   Shuffle your weather cards and deal them evenly to all players. If you’re using the custom builder, create cards like “Sudden Downpour,” “Sunny Streak,” or “Freezing Temps.”

2. **Designate a Weather Forecaster**  
   This person checks the real Philly weather every 15 minutes (or make it up—we won’t judge).

### Rules

- **Rain Alert**: If it’s raining outside (or someone draws a “Rain” card), everyone takes a sip.  
- **Heatwave**: If the temp is above 80°F (27°C) or a “Sunny” card is drawn, last person to put on a pair of sunglasses takes two drinks.  
- **Wind Warning**: If the wind is over 10 mph, everyone must spin around before drinking. The first to fall or stumble has to take a dare challenge.  
- **Snow Day**: If it’s snowing (or “Snow” card is drawn), everyone pairs up for couples drinking games—each team drinks together.  
- **Storm Chaser**: If there’s thunder or lightning, it’s time for NSFW dares. Pick or create one with the custom drinking game builder.  
- **Meme Break**: Every time someone complains about the weather, everyone else shouts a Philly meme (or makes up their own) and the complainer drinks.

#### Bonus Round: Weather Watch

If you’re feeling extra, turn on a live Philly webcam or weather channel. Any time the forecast changes during your game, everyone takes a shot and draws a new dare.
