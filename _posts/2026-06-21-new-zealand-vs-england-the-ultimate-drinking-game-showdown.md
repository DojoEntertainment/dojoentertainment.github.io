## Setting Up The Teams

- **Divide into two teams**: Team New Zealand and Team England. Even numbers work best, but if you’re uneven, let the team with fewer people nominate a “double agent” who plays two rounds per turn.
- Pick a team captain for each side (best if they’re the loudest in the room).

## How To Play

This is all about party drinking games with a competitive edge and plenty of dare challenges. Here’s how to get the chaos started:

### 1. Choose Your Challenge

- On each team’s turn, draw a card from the deck.
- Each suit represents a challenge category:
  - **Hearts**: Dares (think NSFW dares, meme drinking games, or wild challenges)
  - **Diamonds**: Trivia about New Zealand or England (losing team drinks)
  - **Clubs**: Physical challenges (e.g., who can do the best haka or sing “God Save the King” with a mouthful of water)
  - **Spades**: Drinking challenges (e.g., waterfall, shot relay, flip cup)

### 2. Complete or Compete

- Teams go head-to-head. For dares and challenges, both sides must participate.
- The winning team gets a “point” (track on your paper), and the losing team drinks.
- For trivia, the team who answers wrong drinks twice.

### 3. Add Custom Twists

- Use the custom drinking game builder from [Drinking Dojo](https://drinkingdojo.com) to load up new dares, meme questions, or spicy NSFW challenges tailored to your crew.
- Bonus: Add viral drinking games like “Fortnite drinking game” rounds or wedding party games if you’re celebrating something big.

### 4. Rager Finale

- First team to 10 points wins.
- Losing team must perform a legendary group dare — picked by the winners.

## Drinking Game Rules To Remember

- **Safety first**: Know your limits and look out for your mates.
- **No skipping turns**: If you pass on a challenge, your team drinks double.
- **House rules rule**: Got your own wild house party ideas? Add them in!
- **Cheers to the referee**: The captains settle any disputes (with a drink).
