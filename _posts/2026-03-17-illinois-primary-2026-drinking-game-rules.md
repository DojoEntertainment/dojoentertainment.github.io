## Illinois Primary 2026 Drinking Game Rules

Make every speech, awkward handshake, and viral meme count! Here’s how to play:

### Take One Sip When:

- A candidate mentions “change,” “the people,” or “hard work”
- Someone interrupts the moderator
- The camera cuts to enthusiastic supporters in funny hats or costumes
- Election graphics flash across the screen
- You see a meme-worthy facial expression

### Take Two Sips When:

- A candidate dodges a question
- Someone references Illinois landmarks (shoutout to the Bean)
- There’s a technical glitch or awkward silence
- You hear, “As an Illinoisan…”

### Finish Your Drink If:

- A candidate claims victory before official results
- There’s an unexpected upset in any race
- The network calls the winner while you’re mid-sip

### Dare Challenges

Feeling bold? Spice things up with party dares:

- **NSFW dares**: Loser of a trivia round has to DM a candidate on social media with a silly meme
- **Viral drinking games**: If a moment goes viral on Twitter or TikTok, everyone does a shot
- **Election day drinking game twist**: Predict the winner of a close race. If you’re wrong, you have to perform a dare challenge chosen by the group

### Bonus Rounds

- **Couples drinking games**: Partner up and compete for who can predict more correct results—the loser does a dare or dances on camera
- **Wedding party games**: If you’re celebrating at a wedding, set up a primary results bingo board and toast when you hit a row

## Tips for Responsible Fun

- Always know your limits and look out for each other—hydrate, snack, and pace yourself!
- Make sure everyone is of legal drinking age in your country (18+ in Denmark, 19+ in parts of Canada, 21+ in the US, etc.)
- Want to keep the energy wild but the drinks tame? Use non-alcoholic beverages or mix in some water rounds
