## Government Shutdown 2026 Drinking Game

### What You’ll Need

- A group of friends (3+ works best)  
- Your favorite drinks (beer, cocktails, shots—whatever the shutdown mood calls for)  
- A deck of cards (optional, for extra randomness)  
- Access to the latest headlines (TV, phone, or social media)  
- Dare cards or a **custom drinking game builder** (like the ones at [Drinking Dojo](https://drinkingdojo.com))  
- Meme energy and a willingness to get a little wild  

### How To Play

**1. Set Up The Scene**  
Gather everyone around and make sure each person has a drink in hand. Pull up a news stream or a social feed covering the 2026 shutdown updates—think CNN, BBC, Twitter, TikTok, whatever works.

**2. Assign Roles**  
- **Speaker of the House**: The rule-master. Rotates every round.  
- **Senators**: Everyone else. You’re here to play, react, and cause chaos.  

**3. Drinking Triggers**  
Take a sip, gulp, or shot when any of these happen during the news coverage or social scrolling:  

- Someone mentions “gridlock,” “stalemate,” or “crisis”  
- A politician goes viral for a meme-worthy moment (bonus points for sharing the meme)  
- Any official says, “We’re working hard for the people”  
- You hear the phrase “non-essential services”  
- Someone in the group makes an NSFW dare (those who laugh drink)  
- Any news anchor looks visibly stressed  

**4. Dare Challenges**  
Keep a stack of dare cards handy, or use a meme drinking game generator to spice things up. Whenever a shutdown update includes a ridiculous quote or viral meme, the Speaker of the House picks someone to draw a dare. NSFW dares? Only for the brave.

**5. Custom Rule Twist**  
Use a custom drinking game builder to add your own shutdown-specific rules. Example:  
- If a government official’s name is mispronounced, everyone must come up with a new nickname—last to do so drinks.  
- If a shutdown meme hits your group chat, everyone must send a reaction GIF, and the worst one drinks twice.

- **Wedding Party Games Crossover**: If you’re at a wedding (hey, it could happen), add in some couples drinking games and dare challenges for the ultimate party mashup.
