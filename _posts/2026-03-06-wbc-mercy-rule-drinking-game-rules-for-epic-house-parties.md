## What Is the WBC Mercy Rule Drinking Game?

Inspired by the famous “mercy rule” in sports (where the game ends early if one side is getting totally crushed), this drinking game guarantees nobody gets left behind—or, honestly, left too far ahead. It’s all about keeping the playing field fun and fair, with just the right amount of chaos.

Think viral drinking games meets dare challenges, with a twist: if someone’s losing too hard, the mercy rule kicks in and the game flips.

## What You Need

- **A group of 3+ players** (the more, the merrier)
- **Drinks of your choice** (beer, seltzers, cocktails, or fun non-alcoholic options)
- **A deck of cards** or a custom drinking game builder (check out [Drinking Dojo’s custom tools](https://drinkingdojo.com) for next-level setups)
- **Score sheet** (paper, phone, or whiteboard)
- Optional: Props for dares, meme cards, or NSFW dares for the bold

## How To Play WBC Mercy Rule Drinking Game

### 1. Set Up the Game

- Gather everyone around and pick your drinks.
- Decide on the type of dares or meme drinking games you want to mix in (classic, viral, or custom).
- Shuffle your deck and place it in the center.

### 2. Start Rounds

- Players draw cards in turn, each card representing a challenge, question, or dare.
- For every lost round (failed dare, wrong answer, lowest card, etc.), the loser gets a strike (mark on the score sheet).

### 3. The Mercy Rule

- If a player racks up **three strikes** and is falling way behind (while the top player has at least five fewer strikes), the mercy rule kicks in:
  - The player gets a bonus challenge that, if completed, lets them remove one strike.
  - If they fail, they take a penalty drink and everyone else takes a sip in “solidarity.”
- This flips the dynamic and keeps everyone in the game—no one gets totally left out or destroyed (unless that’s the vibe you want).

### 4. Dare Challenges and Meme Twists

- Every mercy rule challenge can be spiced up with viral drinking games or meme drinking games—think Fortnite drinking game dares, NSFW dares, or wild wedding party games for any occasion.
- Examples:
  - **Fortnite Dance-Off:** Loser must perform a Fortnite dance for 20 seconds. Pass = remove a strike.
  - **NSFW Dare:** Spin the bottle for a spicy challenge. Success = mercy, fail = penalty drink.
  - **Wedding Toast:** Give a dramatic fake wedding toast. Nailed it? Mercy granted.

### 5. Winning and Wrapping Up

- Keep playing until a set time or until everyone’s had enough.
- The winner is the player with the fewest strikes at the end—or the last one standing if you’re feeling competitive.
